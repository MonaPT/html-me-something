MPriz's Reading Nook

I have too many favorite books to list. One of my favorite quotes about books is "A mind needs books as a sword needs a whetstone" by Tyrion Lannister from Game of Thrones. One of the biggest reasons why I happen to fall in love with a book is if/when I could relate to any of the major characters of the book which are also inspiring.

Listed below are a few books which I cherish most for different reasons. I happened to read each of them at different phases of my life starting from my childhood. Each of them has had a certain impact on my character, personality, my thought process and my values.

    "Adventures of Tom Sawyer" by Mark Twain
    "Heidi" by Johanna Spyri
    "Sherlock Holmes - The Hound of The Baskervilles" by Sir. Arthur Conan Doyle
    "Harry Potter Series" by J.K. Rowling
    "Pride and Prejudice" by Jane Austen
    "The Perks of Being A Wallflower" by Stephen Chbosky
    "North and South" by Elizabeth Gaskell

"Adventures of Tom Sawyer" by Mark Twain

Adventures of Tom Sawyer was the first ever novel I read in my childhood. It brought out the adventurous streak in me to light. So glad to be living in the state where the author of this novel, Mark Twain grew up.

"Heidi" by Johanna Spyri

I read Heidi in my early teens. Loved the animated version of this story so much that I had an urge to read the book. It's the story of a little girl called Heidi who lives in Swiss Alps. My love for nature, mountains and snow began then. A trip to Swiss Alps is one of my bucket list items.

"Sherlock Holmes - The Hound of The Baskervilles" by Sir. Arthur Conan Doyle

I read this book in my mid teens and immediately took a liking for reading more detective stories, mysteries and thrillers.

"Harry Potter Series" by J.K. Rowling

I read The world famous Harry Potter series (a set of seven books) through my late teens while I was pursuing my Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering. Thats when Fantasy and Fiction have become my all time favorite genres.

"The Perks of Being A Wallflower" by Stephen Chbosky

This is a coming of age epistolary novel, I read in my early twenties which I thoroughly enjoyed. I could relate to one of the characters in the book which is why it was close to my heart.

"Pride and Prejudice" by Jane Austen

I read this book in my late twenties. Fell in love with classics and period dramas ever since.

"North and South" by Elizabeth Gaskell

North and South is a social novel set in 1800's during the time of Industrial Revolution. I admired the plot as well as both the lead characters of this book a great deal.
