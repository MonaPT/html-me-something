MPriz's Reading Nook

I love books. "I love that moment when you open one and sink into it, you can escape from the world, into a story that is way more interesting than your's will ever be." I can totally relate to it. Can't agree more. A good book is a fascinating place to be.

Love this quote "A mind needs books as a sword needs a whetstone" by Tyrion Lannister from Game of Thrones. One of the biggest reasons why I happen to connect with a book is if/when I could relate to any of the major characters of the book. Fantasy, Mystery, Fiction, Adventure/Science Fiction, Historical/Period Drama and Thrillers are my favorite genres.

I have too many of my favorite books to list. Listed below are a few books which I cherish most for different reasons. I happened to read each of them at different phases of my life starting from my childhood. Each of them has had a certain impact on my character, personality, my thought process and my values in life.

    Adventures of Tom Sawyer
    Heidi
    Sherlock Holmes - The Hound of The Baskervilles
    Harry Potter Series
    Pride and Prejudice
    The Perks of Being A Wallflower
    North and South

"Adventures of Tom Sawyer" by Mark Twain

Adventures of Tom Sawyer was the first ever novel I read in my childhood. It unveiled the adventurous streak in me. So glad to be living in the state (Missouri) where the author of this novel, Mark Twain grew up.


"Heidi" by Johanna Spyri

I read Heidi in my early teens. Loved the animated version of this story so much that I had an urge to read the book. It's the story of a little girl called Heidi who lives in Swiss Alps. My great love for nature, woods and mountains, snow and winters began then. A trip to Swiss Alps is one of my bucket list items now.


"Sherlock Holmes - The Hound of The Baskervilles" by Sir. Arthur Conan Doyle

This one here gave me a bone-chilling and nail-biting experience. It is a gripping mystery thriller. I read this book in my mid teens and immediately took a liking for detective stories, mysteries and thrillers.


"Harry Potter Series" by J.K. Rowling

I read The world famous Harry Potter series (set of seven books) through my late teens while I was pursuing my Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering. I must say, I was fascinated. That's when Fantasy and Fiction have become my all time favorite go-to genres. I enjoyed reading the books much more than watching their respective movie adaptations. Looking forward to visiting the Wizarding World of Harry Potter at Universal Studios, Orlando hopefully next year.


"The Perks of Being A Wallflower" by Stephen Chbosky

This is a coming of age epistolary novel, a modern classic, I read in my early twenties which I thoroughly enjoyed. I could relate to one of the main characters in the book which is why it is one of the books that are close to my heart.


"Pride and Prejudice" by Jane Austen

Pride and Prejudice is one such story that is capable of resisting the passage of time. Took a fancy to classics and period dramas ever since I experienced this book.


"North and South" by Elizabeth Gaskell

I read this book recently. North and South is a social novel set in 1800's during the time of Industrial Revolution. I admired the plot as well as both the lead characters of this book a great deal. I can watch the BBC TV series 2004 adaptation of this book a zillion times.
